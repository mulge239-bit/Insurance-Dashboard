# Insurance-Dashboard
Dashboard 1: Insurance Policy Data Analysis Dashboard

Dashboard Insights:

• Claim-wise Analysis: Pie chart displays the proportion of Approved (34.13%), Denied (33.35%), and Pending (32.52%) claims, providing insights into claim handling efficiency.
• Policy Type Distribution: Bar chart shows count of policies across types — Auto, Health, Life, and Property — highlighting Health as the most popular policy.
• Gender-wise Policy Count: Donut chart visualizes gender distribution (Male, Female, Other) to understand demographic patterns among policyholders.
• Payment Status Overview: Compares successful and failed payments to monitor transaction efficiency.
• Premium Growth Rate: Year-wise bar chart showing consistent growth of around 9–10%, peaking at 10.74% in 2016, indicating strong annual premium trends.
• Payment Method Distribution: Analyzes payment preferences (Cash, Bank Transfer, Credit Card) to identify customer transaction behavior.
• Renewal Status Count: Highlights policy renewal trends with counts for Renewed, Pending, and Not Renewed categories.

Tools & Techniques:

• Excel: Designed an interactive dashboard using pivot charts, slicers, filters, and conditional formatting for easy data navigation.
• Power BI: Used for visual comparison of claim ratios, premium growth, and policy distribution.
• MySQL: Extracted, transformed, and cleaned policy and claim data before dashboard visualization.

Project Objective:

• To analyze insurance policy data for insights on customer behavior, claim processing, and financial performance.
• To monitor premium growth and renewal patterns over time.
• To identify top-performing policy types and preferred payment methods.
• To track gender-wise participation and claim outcomes for better policy planning.

Outcome:

The project produced an interactive, data-driven dashboard enabling insurance companies to monitor key metrics in real time. It supports informed decision-making, improves claim management efficiency, and strengthens business strategy through detailed analysis of policy, claim, and customer trends.

Dashboard 2: Weekly Branch Dashboard

Dashboard Insights:

• Cross Sell, New & Renewal Tracking: Visual bars compare Target, Achievement, and Invoice data for each category to measure weekly performance.
• Achievement Metrics: KPI cards display Cross Sell (15.14%), New (17.69%), and Renewal (68.14%) invoice achievements, along with PLCD (Policy Logged & Closed Data) performance.
• Employee Performance Analysis: Charts display the number of client meetings and invoices handled by each account executive, identifying top performers.
• Revenue Funnel: Funnel chart illustrates revenue progression from Qualified Opportunity to Negotiation and Proposal stages.
• Product Distribution: Donut chart presents product-wise opportunity distribution across Fire, Marine, Engineering, Liability, and Employee Benefits.
• Top Revenue Opportunities: Bar charts show top-performing opportunities and open deals, supporting sales prioritization.

Tools & Techniques:

• Power BI: Built a dynamic weekly monitoring dashboard using KPIs, slicers, and comparative visualizations.
• Excel: Integrated pre-cleaned data for consistent visualization and summary reporting.
• SQL: Applied data transformation queries for accurate aggregation of target and achievement values before visualization.

Project Objective:

• To track weekly branch performance across sales, renewals, and cross-sell opportunities.
• To measure achievement percentages against set targets for each account executive.
• To visualize sales funnel and product distribution for better resource allocation.
• To identify top revenue-generating opportunities and improve branch productivity.

Outcome:

The dashboard provides a clear, real-time overview of branch-level sales and renewal activities. It empowers management to evaluate team performance, identify growth areas, and make data-backed decisions to enhance sales efficiency and revenue generation.
